Design Rules for FFF 3D Printing

Workshop Quick Reference Guide

Workshop Goals

This guide covers essential design rules for FFF (Fused Filament Fabrication) 3D printing. These rules apply to any FFF printer and will help you create successful prints the first time.

Quick Reference - Critical Values

  • Minimum Wall Thickness: 0.9 mm (2x line width)
  • Minimum Hole Size: 2 mm diameter
  • Maximum Bridge Length: 10 mm
  • Maximum Overhang Angle: 50° from vertical
  • Clearance (Loose Fit): 0.2 mm
  • Clearance (Tight Fit): 0.1 mm
  • Base Chamfer: 0.3 mm
  • Minimum Feature Size: 1.8 mm

1. Wall Thickness

Minimum: 0.9 mm (2 times extrusion width)

Walls should be at least two extrusions wide for strength and successful printing. Thinner walls may fail or be very weak.

Workshop Tip: Perimeters are the main source of strength. More perimeters = stronger parts.

2. Hole Size

Minimum: 2 mm diameter

Holes smaller than 2 mm may not print accurately. For precise holes of any size, print undersized and drill to final dimension.

Workshop Tip: Vertical holes print better than horizontal holes.

3. Bridging

Maximum Length: 10 mm without support

Horizontal bridges longer than 10 mm should have support structures. You may be able to bridge longer distances depending on material and settings.

Workshop Tip: Design vertical supports into your model or enable supports in your slicer.

4. Overhangs

Maximum Angle: 50° from vertical

Overhangs steeper than 50° from vertical may droop or curl. Modern printers can sometimes handle up to 70°, but 50° is a safe target.

Workshop Tip: The 50° rule means roughly half the layer width can extend beyond the previous layer.

5. Clearance for Fit

Loose Fit: 0.2 mm | Tight Fit: 0.1 mm

When parts need to fit together, add clearance between mating surfaces. Use 0.2 mm for parts that should move easily, 0.1 mm for press-fit parts.

Workshop Tip: Test with a simple calibration print first to dial in clearance for your specific printer.

6. Base Chamfers

Recommended: 0.3 mm (first layer + one layer)

Add a small chamfer to base edges to prevent the "elephant foot" effect where the first layer spreads outward.

Workshop Tip: Only needed when printing without a brim.

7. Corner Rounding

Base Corners: R > 4 mm

Round corners that touch the print bed to reduce warping. Larger radius = better adhesion and less warping stress.

Workshop Tip: Sharp corners concentrate warping forces at one point.

8. Feature Size

Minimum: 1.8 mm (4 times extrusion width)

Small features need to be at least 4 extrusion widths to print reliably. Features smaller than 1.8 mm may not resolve.

Workshop Tip: This applies to pins, posts, tabs, and any small geometric details.

9. Threads

Modeled: M5 or larger | Post-Process: Any size

Modeled threads: Only use for M5 screws or larger. Don't use for horizontal holes.

Post-process methods:

  • Thread tap: Model hole at 90% of thread diameter
  • Self-tapping screw: Model hole at 96% of thread diameter
  • Heat-set insert: Model hole at 98% of insert diameter

Workshop Tip: Post-process threads are more reliable than printed threads.

10. Text & Details

Emboss: >0.9 mm wide, <0.9 mm tall | Engrave: >0.5 mm wide, <0.9 mm deep

On vertical surfaces (walls): Details should be at least 2x nozzle diameter. They'll sag if too tall/deep.

On horizontal surfaces (top/bottom): Make details 0.5mm wide minimum for engraving, 0.9mm for embossing.

Workshop Tip: Bold fonts work better than thin fonts.

Workshop Checklist

  • ✓ Wall Thickness: All walls ≥ 0.9 mm
  • ✓ Holes: All holes ≥ 2 mm
  • ✓ Bridges: All bridges ≤ 10 mm or supported
  • ✓ Overhangs: All overhangs ≤ 50° or supported
  • ✓ Features: All features ≥ 1.8 mm
  • ✓ Base: Chamfers on base edges if no brim
  • ✓ Corners: Round base corners ≥ 4 mm radius
  • ✓ Clearance: 0.1-0.2 mm between mating parts
